Nevermind

by: Nirvana





  • Kurt Cobain - Lead Vocals
  • Dave Grohl - Drums
  • Krist Novoselic - Bass

Nirvana was a groundbreaking American rock band formed in 1987 in Aberdeen, Washington. The band consisted of Kurt Cobain (vocals, guitar), Krist Novoselic (bass), and various drummers over its short but impactful career. Nirvana is often credited with popularizing the grunge genre and spearheading the 1990s alternative rock movement. Their second studio album, "Nevermind," released in 1991, propelled them to worldwide fame with the iconic single "Smells Like Teen Spirit." Cobain's songwriting, marked by raw honesty and angst, resonated with a generation, making Nirvana a symbol of the '90s counterculture. Despite the tragic end to Cobain's life in 1994, Nirvana's influence on music and culture remains profound, and their legacy endures through timeless albums like "Nevermind" and "In Utero."
